Components of Data Communication:

Data communication involves the transmission of data between devices. The key components include the sender, receiver, message, medium, and protocol.

Distributed Processing:

Distributed processing involves the use of multiple computers working in parallel to solve a problem by dividing tasks among them.

Line Configuration, Topology:

Line Configuration: Refers to the arrangement of communication channels, such as point-to-point or multipoint.

Topology: Describes the physical or logical layout of a network, including bus, star, ring, etc.

Transmission Mode:

Transmission mode determines the direction of data flow between devices. It can be simplex, half-duplex, or full-duplex.

Categories of Networks:

Networks can be categorized based on their size and purpose, such as LANs (Local Area Networks), WANs (Wide Area Networks), and MANs (Metropolitan Area Networks).

OSI and TCP/IP Models:

The OSI (Open Systems Interconnection) and TCP/IP models are frameworks defining network protocols and communication layers.

Transmission Media: Guided and Unguided:

Guided: Includes cables and wires, such as twisted pair and fiber optics.

Unguided: Involves wireless communication mediums, such as radio waves and microwaves.

Attenuation, Distortion, Noise, Throughput:

Attenuation: Weakening of signals over distance.

Distortion: Alteration of the signal shape.

Noise: Unwanted signals that interfere with communication.

Throughput: Actual data transfer rate.

Propagation Speed and Time, Wavelength, Shannon Capacity:

Propagation Speed: Speed at which signals travel.

Time: Duration for a signal to travel from sender to receiver.

Wavelength: Distance between successive peaks of a wave.

Shannon Capacity: Maximum data rate for reliable communication.
